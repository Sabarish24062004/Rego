The Standard

What is OSCAL — and why does it matter?

OSCAL (Open Security Controls Assessment Language) is a NIST-developed open standard that makes security documentation machine-readable, interoperable, and continuously verifiable. RegO is built OSCAL-native from the ground up.

📄

Machine-Readable

OSCAL replaces static Word documents and spreadsheets with structured JSON/XML/YAML that can be ingested, validated, and acted on programmatically.

🔄

Always Current

Unlike point-in-time audits, OSCAL documents are continuously updated as your environment changes — giving authorizing officials a live view of your compliance posture.

🏛️

Government-Backed

OSCAL is mandated by NIST and adopted by FedRAMP. It is the future of ATO documentation for all Federal agencies and Defense contractors.

🔗

Interoperable

OSCAL artifacts from RegO are compatible with any OSCAL-capable tool, assessor, or agency system — eliminating vendor lock-in for your compliance data.
